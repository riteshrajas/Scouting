Formula Decoder: What does (...) mean?

x = (MTM)-1 MT s

The parentheses (...) group the "Schedule" and the "Transpose" together so we can invert them as one unit. Think of it like algebra: you have to calculate $(2 + 2) \times 5$ before you get $20$. Here is the breakdown:

M The Schedule: A giant grid of 0s and 1s representing who played in which match.
MT Transpose: We flip the grid sideways. This lines up teams against matches.
(MTM) The Connection: This multiplication creates a map of "Who played with whom?"
-1 The Inverse: This is the "Solver." It attempts to untangle the web of alliances to find individual values.

1. Instability (Early Quals)

The Issue: With few matches, the "Solver" ($^{-1}$) doesn't have enough data to untangle the teams. It guesses wildly.

2. No Signal Separation

The Issue: OPR only looks at the Final Score ($s$). It cannot see how the points were scored (Auto vs Teleop).

3. False Independence (Synergy)

The Issue: OPR assumes you are exactly the same robot in every match. It cannot handle "Synergy" (e.g., passing pieces). If you score +20 points only when paired with a specific partner, OPR gets confused.

What happened?
The OPR formula saw the high scores from the synergy matches and averaged them into both teams' individual values.

1. "The Smearing Effect": It gave both teams a "partial credit" for the synergy bonus, permanently inflating their stats.
2. Playing Apart: Because their stats are inflated, OPR over-estimates them when they play with others.
3. Playing Together: Even though their stats are inflated, OPR still under-estimates the super-alliance because it treats the bonus as linear, not exponential.
